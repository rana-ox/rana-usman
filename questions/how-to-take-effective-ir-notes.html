How to Take Effective Notes

Effective note-taking is the secret to retaining complex information. For subjects like IR, it requires shifting from passive writing to active synthesis.

★
The Cornell Method Divide your page into three sections: "Cues" (left), "Notes" (right), and "Summary" (bottom). This forces you to review and condense your thoughts immediately.
★
Mind Mapping for IR Instead of linear lists, use mind maps to connect different concepts—like how a trade war in the US affects local prices in Pakistan.
★
Active Review (24-Hour Rule) Review your notes within 24 hours of taking them. This simple act can increase information retention by up to 80%.
"Do not record everything you hear. Instead, record what you understand."
