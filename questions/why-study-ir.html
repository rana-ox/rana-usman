Why Study International Relations?

International Relations (IR) provides the tools to decipher the complex web of global politics, economics, and law. It is the essential degree for anyone looking to understand how the world actually works.

1
Decoding Global Dynamics Studying IR allows you to look beyond headlines to understand the structural causes of war, peace, and economic shifts.
2
Developing a Multi-Disciplinary Edge You don't just study politics; you dive into history, economics, law, and sociology, making you a well-rounded intellectual.
3
High-Impact Career Paths From the Foreign Office and United Nations to international NGOs and corporate risk analysis, the career scope is vast and prestigious.
